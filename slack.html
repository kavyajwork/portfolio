TL;DR

Slack is efficient — but it was never designed for human presence. I designed Circles: a new layer inside Slack that makes ambient collaboration possible for hybrid teams without adding noise.

Context

Slack is built for communication.
Not for presence.

Remote work gave us tools to stay connected — but not to feel present. There's a gap between sending a message and sharing a moment. Between being online and feeling part of a team.

Being connected

  • Slack threads & channels
  • Scheduled meetings
  • Status updates
  • Intentional communication

Feeling present

  • Knowing someone's around
  • Joining spontaneously
  • Casual visibility
  • Ambient awareness

Remote work creates

01

Sending messages proves you're active. It doesn't make you feel part of the room.

02

03

04

Built for task communication. Not for the casual, ambient layer that makes people feel like a team.

The Solution

Slack Circles — a new layer that makes
ambient collaboration possible.

Circles lives inside Slack and introduces three interlocking features — each designed to reduce the effort of being visible while increasing the warmth of being part of a team.

Feature 01 · Live Sessions

"I'm here, working on this."
Joinable. No scheduling required.

Live lets anyone on the team broadcast a lightweight session — what they're working on, who's welcome to join. It appears in the Circles sidebar and vanishes when you leave.

Before

Schedule a meeting. Send an invite. Wait. Hope people show up.

→

After

Open a Live. Teammates see it. They join if they want. No pressure.

The Live modal — lightweight, casual, no scheduling needed.

Live sessions appear in the Circles feed — teammates can see who's broadcasting and join with one click.

Feature 02 · Highlights

Quick, casual updates.
Visible for 3 days.

Highlights are low-stakes status posts — a thought, a photo, a moment from your day. Not a report. Not a deliverable. Just a window into what you're doing, visible to your team without clogging channels.

Design reasoning

The 3-day visibility window was a deliberate choice. Permanent posts create pressure to post "worthy" content. Ephemeral posts (24hr) don't give passive readers enough time to catch up. 3 days hits the sweet spot — casual enough to share freely, persistent enough to be seen.

Highlights in context — sitting alongside Live sessions in the Circles feed.

Feature 03 · Circles Feed

Live + async, together.
One surface. Meaningful activity.

The Circles feed combines Live sessions, Highlights, and Upcoming events into a single ambient view. Not a notification centre. Not a channel. A gentle, scrollable picture of what your team is up to — right now and recently.

My teams

See activity from people you work with directly — filtered for signal, not noise.

Everyone

Browse company-wide — for new joiners like Sarah who want to understand the broader culture.

Iterations

Four changes that made
a real difference.

"Post" → "Share Highlight"

Testing showed "Post" felt like publishing. "Share Highlight" felt casual and intentional about the 3-day window.

No visibility timer → 3-day visibility added

Without a timer, users worried about "cluttering" the feed. Adding "visible for 3 days" immediately reduced posting anxiety.

Circles = channel? → Circles = dedicated sidebar space

3 of 5 users initially thought Circles was a Slack channel. Added a dedicated sidebar item and onboarding tooltip to clarify.

Live felt formal → Live felt like a casual open door

Changed copy from "Start a meeting" to "Go Live" and softened button style. The tone shift measurably reduced hesitation to start a session.

Impact

Not more tools.
More presence.

Circles doesn't replace Slack channels or DMs. It adds the layer that was always missing — the ambient, human layer where teams feel like teams, not just groups of people sharing a workspace.

◎

Better awareness

Team activity is visible without effort — passively surfaced, not actively broadcast.

↗

Faster collaboration

Live sessions enable spontaneous syncs without the overhead of scheduling.

✦

Reduced isolation

Passive visibility makes remote workers feel seen — even without a single DM sent.

Reflection

"The problem isn't a lack of tools.
It's a lack of presence."

The most interesting discovery in this project wasn't a feature — it was a reframe. Teams don't need more ways to communicate. They need a lower-effort way to exist together. Circles is designed around that insight: presence before communication, ambient before intentional.

If I were taking this further, I'd want to measure whether Highlights actually reduce isolation scores over time — and whether Live sessions correlate with higher spontaneous collaboration rates. The hypothesis is strong. The longitudinal data would make it real.
